Essential Composition Guidelines

Use The Rule Of Thirds

The rule of thirds is loosely based on a tic-tac-toe grid that we can imagine overlaying our photograph. Placing the most important aspect of the image on one of the intersecting points makes the image more appealing to the human eye.

Use Symmetry

When our subject is symmetrical, meaning that the left side is equal, or almost equal to the right side, its best to compose our photo as straight on as possible.

Light and shadow

Light and shadow plays a major role in photography. Having both creates a balance. When light is absent, shadows can help the eye to a specific point creating a composition. Shadows are also used as a hint of drama which highlight an image.

Patience & Positioning

Sometimes we have to be patient when trying to compose an image. What happens if the subject never move into the right place? Simple. We change our position.

Perspective

Do not be afraid to change your perspective and shooting angle to achieve the composition you are aiming for. For example photographing from above helps your subject appear smaller and more vulnerable.

Practice makes it better

The best advice. Practice! Shoot a lot. Discover what you need to work on and go after mastering that task. Do not be afraid to fail.
